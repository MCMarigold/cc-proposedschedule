✓ Pros
  • +Weekday late-morning coverage roughly doubles — Wed 11am goes from 1–2 agents to 3–5, cutting per-agent load from 37.1 → 18.6 min/agent.
  • +Weekday evening handoff (~6pm) sees the same kind of relief — Fri 5pm improves from 35.9 → 18.0 min/agent.
  • +Friday now works better for all three of Maggie's Friday 1-on-1s — Alicia's new Friday shift means she's on duty as backup during Deb's 11am and Sara's 2pm meetings (Fri 11am 29.9 → 15.0 min/agent; Fri 2pm 32.5 → 16.3 min/agent), and moving Alicia's own 1-on-1 to Friday noon (since she's now working that day) lands on the same 15.8 min/agent either way — no coverage difference between the two schedules for her meeting slot.
  • +No new admin cost — the schedule changes are shift-time adjustments only, not added headcount.
✕ Cons
  • –Sunday 12pm–7pm drops from 2 agents to 1, since Alicia's Sunday shift moves to Friday — Sunday 5pm goes from 24.1 → 48.2 min/agent, the single largest regression anywhere on the map.
  • –7pm Mon–Thu drops from Alicia + Monique (and Sara on Mondays) down to Monique alone every night, because Alicia's weekday shift now ends at 7pm instead of 8pm.
  • –These are real trade-offs, not just modeling noise — same total workload, genuinely thinner coverage in both windows.

Bottom line

The proposed schedule is a net improvement on weekday coverage, especially the late-morning and evening handoff windows that are hardest to staff today. But it isn't a clean win — it trades a well-covered Sunday afternoon for better weekday balance. Worth deciding explicitly whether that trade is acceptable, or whether a modified version (keeping Alicia on Sunday, reverting Sara's Friday hours) is worth considering instead.
